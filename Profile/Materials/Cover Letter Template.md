# Cover Letter Template

> [!info] Reusable Skeleton
> Replace `{{placeholders}}`. Keep it to **~250–350 words**. Only send when the application asks for one or it clearly helps.

---

> [!tip] Structure
> 1. **Opening** — introduce yourself, the role, and the company
> 2. **Experience** — one quantified accomplishment tied to the role
> 3. **Project** — one project matching the job's stack (optional)
> 4. **Closing** — express interest, thank them

---

Dear {{Hiring Team / Name}},

I'm a junior computer science student at Texas A&M University applying for the =={{role}}== internship at =={{Company}}== for Fall 2026. {{One sentence on why this company specifically — a product, value, or team that genuinely interests you.}}

As a student technician at the Texas A&M TechHub, I built and maintain a full-stack delivery management system (Flask, React, MySQL) that processes 150+ orders a month at 99.8% uptime — real operational software that staff depend on daily. {{Second sentence tying a skill from that work to what this role needs.}}

{{Optional paragraph: a project that maps to the job's stack — e.g. Project SHADE (LSTM/ML pipeline) or FlightPath (Flask/REST/SQL). Name the technologies.}}

I'd welcome the chance to contribute to {{team/product}} and grow as an engineer at {{Company}}. Thank you for your consideration.

Sincerely,

==Kyler Cao==

---

> [!warning] Per-Application Checklist
> - [ ] Company name correct everywhere
> - [ ] Role title matches the posting exactly
> - [ ] The ==why this company== line is specific, not generic
> - [ ] One quantified accomplishment included
> - [ ] Proofread

> [!example] Quantified Wins (pick the best fit)
> - `Processes 150+ orders/mo at 99.8% uptime`
> - `Reduced processing time 40% via async task queue`
> - `Scraped & structured 100K+ job listings daily`

> [!note] When to Skip
> If the application portal has a text area labeled "Cover Letter" or "Additional Information", use a shorter version (1 paragraph, 100-150 words). If there's no explicit cover letter upload or field, skip it — attaching one when not asked can hurt more than help.
