# Cover Letter Template

> Reusable skeleton. Replace `{{placeholders}}`. Keep it to ~250-350 words. Only send when the application asks for one or it clearly helps.

---

Dear {{Hiring Team / Name}},

I'm a junior computer science student at Texas A&M University applying for the {{role}} internship at {{Company}} for Fall 2026. {{One sentence on why this company specifically — a product, value, or team that genuinely interests you.}}

As a student technician at the Texas A&M TechHub, I built and maintain a full-stack delivery management system (Flask, React, MySQL) that processes 150+ orders a month at 99.8% uptime — real operational software that staff depend on daily. {{Second sentence tying a skill from that work to what this role needs — e.g. debugging, systems reliability, working with structured data.}}

{{Optional paragraph: a project that maps to the job's stack — e.g. Project SHADE (LSTM/ML pipeline) or FlightPath (Flask/REST/SQL). Name the technologies.}}

I'd welcome the chance to contribute to {{team/product}} and grow as an engineer at {{Company}}. Thank you for your consideration.

Sincerely,
Kyler Cao

## Per-application checklist
- [ ] Company name correct everywhere
- [ ] Role title matches the posting exactly
- [ ] The "why this company" line is specific, not generic
- [ ] One quantified accomplishment included
